# Compliance Rules for Wine Trading

## General Compliance Requirements

### Confidence Thresholds
- All BUY and SELL recommendations must have a minimum confidence score of 0.6
- High-risk recommendations (negative expected ROI) require confidence >= 0.75
- HOLD recommendations have no minimum confidence requirement

### Portfolio Concentration Limits
- No single asset should represent more than 20% of total portfolio value
- Regional concentration: No single region should exceed 40% of portfolio value
- Vintage concentration: No single vintage should exceed 30% of portfolio value

### Risk Management
- Maximum position size: 10% of total portfolio value per trade
- Stop-loss threshold: Automatic sell if position loses more than 15% of purchase value
- Diversification requirement: Minimum of 3 different assets in portfolio

## Arbitrage-Specific Rules

### Volume Requirements
- Minimum volume available: 1 bottle
- Maximum volume per arbitrage trade: 10 bottles
- Verify liquidity before executing arbitrage opportunities

### Price Validation
- Buy price must be at least 5% lower than sell price
- Expected profit must exceed transaction costs by at least 2%
- Confidence score for arbitrage must be >= 0.7

## Regional Compliance

### Cross-Border Trading
- Verify import/export regulations for target regions
- Check tax implications for cross-region trades
- Ensure proper documentation for international shipments

### Regional Restrictions
- Some regions may have restrictions on wine trading
- Verify local licensing requirements
- Check age restrictions and legal drinking age requirements

## Documentation Requirements

### Required Documents
- Purchase receipts
- Shipping documentation
- Insurance certificates
- Compliance certificates (where applicable)

### Record Keeping
- Maintain records for all trades for minimum 7 years
- Document all compliance checks performed
- Keep audit trail of all decisions
